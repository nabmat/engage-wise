Case of the week

"Do messages of scarcity increase trial recruitment?"

Caroline J. Free, Elizabeth Hoile, Rosemary Knight, Steven Robertson, Karen M. Devries (2011)

Key finding: Using scarcity messages in recruitment (e.g., "only 300 places left") increased trial consent rates by 50% compared to standard messages.

"Evaluating the benefits of a patient information video during the informed consent process"

Julie Weston, Mary Hannah, Julia Downes (1997)

Key finding: Using informational videos during consent increased willingness to participate from 35.4% to 61.9%, and improved long-term information retention.

"Recruitment of Hispanic women to the Women's Health Initiative: the case of Embajadoras in Arizona"

Linda K. Larkey et al. (2002)

Key finding: Trained Hispanic lay advocates were 7.8 times more likely to successfully refer participants compared to untrained advocates, demonstrating the power of culturally-aligned recruitment.
