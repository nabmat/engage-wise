About Engage+

Our mission is to provide the most context-relevant, evidence-based resource for improving recruitment, retention, and representation in health and care research. We do this by maintaining high editorial standards and a transparent approach to curating, reviewing, and presenting scientific literature. Our team includes methodologists, behavioural scientists, and researchers with lived experience in trial delivery — all of whom are committed to surfacing insights that are both rigorous and practical.

We do not promote off-the-shelf solutions. Instead, Intelligent Insights offers a clear and nuanced synthesis of the peer-reviewed research, tailored to your study's design, population, and setting. Our aim is to help you design participant-centred research — not just to improve recruitment metrics, but to strengthen quality, ethics, and equity.

We categorically avoid sensationalism, unsupported claims, or inflated promises. Our users are trial managers, clinical academics, and operational leads who require accurate, up-to-date, and actionable information. We respect that responsibility.

Independence and Transparency

We do not accept paid content or advertising, and our research outputs are not influenced by external sponsors. All literature reviews, recommendations, and recommendations are produced internally or by commissioned academics under strict editorial independence. No commercial entity may alter or influence the findings we publish.

Our editorial team declares no financial conflicts of interest related to participant recruitment services or platforms. Where we collaborate with trusted partners (e.g., designers or vendors), this is clearly indicated, and recommendations are based on quality, not commission.

Content Development and Review

Every strategy or intervention featured on Intelligent Insights is:

  • Systematically sourced from published, peer-reviewed literature.
  • Reviewed by a human behavioural scientist or clinical trials methodologist.
  • Mapped to relevant study contexts (e.g., rare disease, primary care, underserved populations).

Each summary goes through multiple stages of quality assurance — including scoping, extraction, synthesis, and readability checks. We do not summarise content until full papers are reviewed in context.

As Intelligent Insights grows, we will invite community input to strengthen our content and transparency, including version histories, user reviews, and direct links to source papers.

Updates and Corrections

We regularly review and update our database to reflect new evidence, guideline changes, and user feedback. If you believe you've found an error or wish to challenge an interpretation, we welcome your input.

Corrections are made transparently and promptly, and our aim is to become the most trusted, accurate, and context-sensitive source of recruitment evidence in clinical research.
